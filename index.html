Choose Your Own Adventure

Options for the family to split up, explore, or tackle different paces.

Athens: Marketplaces or Michelin Stars?

Option A (Culinary Exploration): Visit ERGON House, a unique space where the ground floor is a massive marketplace bursting with Greek produce, artisanal olive oils, and local treats.

Option B (Fine Dining): Book a table at Nolan near Syntagma Square. It holds a Michelin Bib Gourmand and features incredible Mediterranean dishes with an Asian twist (like their famous Mediterranean tuna with ponzu).

Option C (The Birth of Democracy): Explore the Ancient Agora and its museum. Look for the "ostraca"—the inscribed ceramic shards ancient citizens used to vote to exile politicians!

Santorini: Fortresses vs. Open-Air Cinema

Option A (History & Views): Hike out to the medieval ruins of Skaros Rock or drive up to Ancient Thera, the classical mountaintop city that once housed a Ptolemaic garrison.

Option B (The Local Vibe): Escape the caldera crowds with dinner at Metaxí Mas in the quiet village of Exo Gonia (tucked behind the Agios Charalambos church). Afterwards, take a short 5-minute drive down to the open-air cinema in Kamari for a late-night movie.

Paros: Naoussa Nights or Ancient Trails?

Option A (See and Be Seen): Head into Naoussa. We can try to snag a harbor-front table at the famous Barbarossa for high-end seafood, then wander the alleys for lukumades (Greek donuts with honey or chocolate).

Option B (Daytime Adventure): Drive to the ancient village of Lefkes and hike the 1,000-year-old paved Byzantine Road down to Prodromos.

Berlin: Spring Festivals & Palaces

Option A (Late May Festivals): Since we are visiting in late May, we are perfectly timed for the massive Carnival of Cultures (May 22-25) or the exhibitions during Berlin Design Week (May 28-31).

Option B (Train Trip): Catch a 30-minute train from the central station to Potsdam to spend the morning exploring Prussian palaces and UNESCO-listed parks.

Option C (Bib Gourmand Dining): Try Berlin's renowned casual fine-dining scene at places like Funky Fisch or Barra, or grab an authentic Adana Kebab wrapped in house-made lavash.

Site Histories

The Acropolis & The Ancient Agora

Legend holds that Athena and Poseidon competed for the patronage of Athens right on the Acropolis. Poseidon struck the rock to create a saltwater spring, but Athena offered the first olive tree and won. Below the Acropolis lies the Ancient Agora, the beating commercial and political heart of the city where democracy was practically invented, surviving destruction by the Persians, Romans, and Herulians.

Santorini: Volcanoes & Garrisons

The island's famous crescent shape is the scar of the Minoan eruption (around 1600 BC), one of the largest volcanic events in recorded history that likely inspired the myth of Atlantis. Centuries later, the high, defensible ridges were settled again, most notably at Ancient Thera, a mountaintop city that served as a strategic garrison for the Ptolemaic kings of Egypt.

Paros: The Island of Flawless Marble

In antiquity, Paros was famous for "Parian lithos" (marble). The ancient underground quarries at Marathi produced the flawless, semi-transparent white stone used to carve masterpieces like the Venus de Milo and the Nike of Paionios. The island is also famous for the Parian Chronicle, a massive marble stele engraved with a timeline of Greek history and mythology.

Berlin: The Airlift & The Wall

Following World War II, a blockaded Berlin was sustained by "Operation Vittles" (the Berlin Airlift)—a massive 15-month humanitarian air campaign that delivered over 2.3 million tons of supplies. The city was later physically divided by the Berlin Wall. Today, the Gedenkstätte Berliner Mauer preserves the history of that division right in the heart of the reunited capital.
