Site Histories

The Acropolis & Parthenon

Rising above Athens, the Acropolis was the ancient city's religious center and fortress. The crown jewel, the Parthenon, was completed in 438 BC at the height of the Athenian Empire. Dedicated to Athena, the patron goddess of the city, it is considered the pinnacle of Doric architecture. Interestingly, it has served as a Greek temple, a Christian church, and an Islamic mosque throughout its history.

The Santorini Caldera

The crescent shape of Santorini is the result of one of the largest volcanic eruptions in recorded history—the Minoan eruption (around 1600 BC). The center of the island collapsed into the sea, creating the submerged caldera we see today. Many historians believe this cataclysmic event inspired the legend of the lost city of Atlantis.

Parian Marble

While Paros is now famous for its beaches and villages like Naoussa, in antiquity, its primary export was stone. "Parian marble" was highly prized by ancient Greek sculptors for its flawless white, semi-transparent quality. Masterpieces like the Venus de Milo and the Winged Victory of Samothrace were carved from marble quarried on this very island.
