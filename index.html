Choose Your Own Adventure

Options to split the family, match different energy levels, or fill free afternoons.

Athens

The Antikythera Mechanism

For Erin: Visit the National Archaeological Museum to see the world's first analog computer. Used to predict astronomical positions with terrifying precision, it is a masterclass in ancient mechanical engineering.

📍 Map to Museum

Voukourestiou Street & Greek Luxury

For Lauren: Wander down Voukourestiou Street, Athens' high-end retail artery. Pop into flagship stores for Greek luxury brands like Zeus+Dione to see how heritage styling is marketed globally.

📍 Map to Voukourestiou St

Santorini

Skaros Rock & Ancient Thera

Hike out to the medieval Venetian ruins of Skaros Rock on the caldera rim, or drive up to Ancient Thera—a mountaintop city perched at 360m. The ancient walls survive because they brilliantly utilize the natural basalt contours.

📍 Map to Skaros Rock | 📍 Map to Ancient Thera

Kamari Open-Air Cinema

Escape the caldera crowds. After a quiet dinner in Exo Gonia, take a short drive down the hill to Kamari's famous open-air cinema for a late-night film under the stars with a cocktail.

📍 Map to Cinema

Paros

Lefkes & the Byzantine Road

Drive to the mountain village of Lefkes and hike the 1,000-year-old marble-paved Byzantine Road down through olive groves to Prodromos. The ancient engineering—designed to channel rainwater and resist erosion—is still entirely functional.

📍 Map to Trailhead

Naoussa Harbour & Loukoumades

Head into Naoussa in the early evening. Secure a harbor-front table for high-end seafood, then wander the whitewashed alleys for freshly fried loukoumades (Greek donuts with honey and crushed pistachios).

📍 Map to Naoussa

Berlin

Design Week & Bikini Berlin

For Lauren: Berlin Design Week runs during your stay. Combine it with a visit to Bikini Berlin, the world's first "concept mall." Instead of chain stores, it features temporary "boxes" for emerging designers to launch brands.

📍 Map to Bikini Berlin

Day Trip to Potsdam Palaces

A 30-minute S-Bahn ride from the center brings you to Sanssouci, Frederick the Great's UNESCO-listed Baroque palace. The terraced vineyard gardens and rococo teahouse feel worlds away from urban Berlin.

📍 Map to Potsdam

Historical Context

The stories behind the stones — arranged to deepen what you'll see in person.

Classical Period

Athens: The Birth of Democracy

Legend holds that Athena and Poseidon competed for Athens on this rock. Below, the Ancient Agora was the beating commercial and democratic heart of the ancient world. It was here the idea of citizens governing themselves was tested, refined, and exported.

Bronze Age

Santorini: Volcanic Catastrophe

The island's crescent shape is the scar of the Minoan eruption (c. 1600 BC)—one of the largest volcanic events in human history. It collapsed the center of the island into the sea and likely inspired the myth of Atlantis.

Antiquity to Present

Paros: Flawless Marble

In antiquity, Paros was famous for "Parian lithos," a uniquely semi-transparent white marble used for masterpieces like the Venus de Milo. Today, its fishing villages like Naoussa represent the pinnacle of Cycladic island architecture.

Post-WWII to Reunification

Berlin: The Airlift & The Wall

After WWII left Berlin divided, the Soviets blockaded access in 1948. Western powers sustained the city entirely by air in "Operation Vittles." Later, the Berlin Wall physically severed the city, leaving a legacy of division and eventual reunification.
