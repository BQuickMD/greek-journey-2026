Choose Your Own Adventure

Options to split the family, match different energy levels, or fill free afternoons. Click any card to expand.

Athens Democracy, Ruins & Agora Markets
A

ERGON House Agora

The ground floor is a spectacular marketplace packed with Greek olive oils, artisanal cheeses, and cured meats — essentially a living, shoppable catalog of Greek produce. The café upstairs turns the same ingredients into refined small plates. A natural for Lauren to browse the intersection of food, branding, and retail.

B

The Ancient Agora & Museum

The beating commercial and political heart of classical Athens — where the concept of democratic debate was literally invented. Look for the "ostraca," shards of pottery Athenians used to vote for politicians' exile. The reconstructed Stoa of Attalos houses a superb collection. For Erin: the ancient engineering of the water clock (klepsydra) is worth finding.

C

Plaka & Monastiraki Wander

The oldest continuously inhabited neighborhood in Athens. Wind through marble-cobbled alleys, past Byzantine-era churches with Ottoman minarets, down into Monastiraki's flea market, and finish with a coffee overlooking the Roman Agora. At dusk, the Acropolis lights up above the rooftops — one of travel's great visual rewards.

Santorini Fortresses, Volcanoes & Open-Air Cinema
A

Skaros Rock & Ancient Thera

Hike out to the medieval Venetian ruins of Skaros Rock on the caldera rim, or drive up to Ancient Thera — a mountaintop city perched at 360m that served as a Ptolemaic military garrison and later a Byzantine stronghold. The views extend to neighboring islands and make the ruins feel genuinely remote. Erin would appreciate the engineering: the walls survive in part because they follow the natural basalt contours.

B

Exo Gonia & Kamari Open-Air Cinema

Escape the caldera crowds entirely — dinner at Metaxí Mas in the quiet inland village of Exo Gonia (tucked behind Agios Charalambos church; beloved by locals for its moussaka and house wine). Then a 5-minute drive down to Kamari's open-air cinema for a late-night film under the stars. One of the Aegean's most charming evenings.

Paros Naoussa Harbour Nights vs Byzantine Trails
A

Naoussa Harbour & Lakkoumades

Head into Naoussa in the early evening: try to secure a harbor-front table at Barbarossa for high-end seafood, or wander to one of the smaller alley tavernas. End with loukoumades — hot fried dough with honey and crushed pistachios — from a street vendor. The harbor lights reflect off the former Venetian fortress ruins into the water. A very good evening.

B

Lefkes & the Byzantine Road

Drive to the mountain village of Lefkes and hike the 1,000-year-old marble-paved Byzantine Road (roughly 3.5km) down through olive groves to Prodromos. The path was the island's main trade artery for centuries. Gentle descent, spectacular views, and almost no tourists. For Erin: the medieval engineering of the path — designed to channel rainwater and resist erosion — is still entirely functional.

C

Piso Livadi & Marble Quarry

The village of Piso Livadi is quieter than Naoussa and has excellent waterfront seafood at Markakis — a small, authentic place by fishing boats using strictly local ingredients. Nearby, the ancient Marathi marble quarries (source of the marble for the Venus de Milo) can be visited; the underground tunnels are eerie and extraordinary.

Berlin Design Week, Palaces & Cold War History
A

Berlin Design Week (May 28–31)

The timing is perfect: Berlin Design Week runs precisely during your stay, with exhibitions, studios, and showrooms open across Mitte, Prenzlauer Berg, and Kreuzberg. For Lauren — fashion, product design, packaging, branding, and retail concepts converge here in a way few European cities match. Check the program at designweek.berlin; some events require advance registration.

B

Day Trip to Potsdam

A 30-minute S-Bahn ride from the center brings you to Sanssouci, Frederick the Great's UNESCO-listed Baroque palace and park complex. The terraced vineyard gardens, rococo Chinese teahouse, and sprawling parkland feel worlds away from the city. The New Palace at the far end of the park is the most dramatically scaled royal building in Germany outside Munich. Half a day is enough; arrive by 9am before coach groups.

C

Berlin Wall Memorial & Checkpoint Charlie

The Gedenkstätte Berliner Mauer (Wall Memorial) on Bernauer Strasse preserves a full original section with watchtower, death strip, and escape tunnels documented in haunting detail — far more affecting than the touristy Checkpoint Charlie. The Hamburger Bahnhof contemporary art museum is a short walk. Plan for a few hours; the memorial is unexpectedly powerful.

Dining Guide

Michelin Bib Gourmand picks, local gems, and good-value finds — the philosophy is great ingredients, honest cooking, no tourist markup.

🏛️

Athens

Nolan Bib Gourmand

Mediterranean · Asian Fusion

Near Syntagma Square, Nolan earns its Bib Gourmand with inventive dishes that blend Greek ingredients with East Asian technique — the Mediterranean tuna with ponzu and the slow-cooked lamb with miso are signatures. Exceptional value for the quality.

Book ahead; this is one of the harder reservations in Athens.

Fine Mess Smokehouse Bib Gourmand

American BBQ · Greek

An unlikely Athens Bib Gourmand — proper American-style smoked meats that happen to use exceptional Greek produce. The beef ribs and the house-smoked pulled pork with local herbs are the draw. Casual, loud, and very satisfying.

No reservations taken; arrive before 7pm or wait.

Cerdo Negro 1985 Bib Gourmand

Modern Spanish · Greek

A Michelin Bib in the Psyrri neighborhood with a strong emphasis on the grill and Iberian pork cuts — cooked over Greek charcoal. The menu is small and changes constantly, and the wine list leans natural Greek producers.

Best for dinner; the neighborhood comes alive after 9pm.

Merceri Michelin Rec.

Modern Greek

In a quiet Thissio side street near the Herakleidon Museum, run by two chefs who honed their craft at luxury Santorini hotels. Contemporary Greek with international flair — a tasting menu or à la carte are both available, with excellent wine pairing options. Reliably excellent.

Tables outside on the quiet street are lovely in May. The amberjack and tuna sashimi have been called "out of this world" by recent guests.

ERGON House Local Gem

Greek Marketplace · All-day

Equal parts market, restaurant, and hotel. The ground-floor agora stocks the best curated selection of Greek pantry in Athens — single-estate oils, mountain honey, artisanal pasta. Upstairs, the kitchen turns those same ingredients into beautiful, ingredient-forward plates.

Go for breakfast or lunch; less expensive than dinner and the market is at its liveliest.

🌋

Santorini

Metaxí Mas Hidden Gem

Traditional Greek Taverna

The standout value dining on the island — tucked behind the Agios Charalambos church in the inland village of Exo Gonia, well away from the caldera tourist trail. Home-cooked moussaka, lamb chops, and the complimentary cheesecake to finish are all exceptional. Over 6,200 reviews averaging 4.7 stars.

Ask for a table on the terrace. Combine with the Kamari open-air cinema afterwards — it's a 5-minute drive down the hill.

Kokkalo Local Gem

Traditional Greek · Fira

A cozy spot in Fira with a menu of local specialties — the tomato fritters (tomatokeftedes) and baked feta in pie crust are the must-orders. Comfortable, honest, and a fraction of caldera-view restaurant prices.

Order the local white wine — Santorini's assyrtiko is a revelation at this altitude.

Salt & Pepper Local Favorite

Seafood · Family-run · Fira

A family-run restaurant praised for its fresh seafood, generous portions, and warm atmosphere. The owner's hospitality and complimentary dessert have become part of the charm. 4.8 stars from over 1,100 reviews.

Great for a relaxed lunch before an afternoon hike or winery visit.

⚓

Paros

Barbarossa Local Gem

Seafood · Naoussa Harbour

The famous harbor-front table in Naoussa — celebrated for high-end seafood and one of the island's most atmospheric settings, with the Venetian fortress ruins reflected in the water behind you. Crowded in peak season; worth the visit despite the premium.

Walk the alleys after dinner for loukoumades (fried dough with honey and crushed pistachios) from the street vendor near the old kastro gate.

Markakis Hidden Gem

Seafood · Piso Livadi

A small, authentic waterfront spot in the quieter village of Piso Livadi, facing the fishing boats. Strictly local ingredients, simple honest preparation. The kind of place where the fish was in the water that morning. Their deli in nearby Márpissa sells local products and does takeout.

Ideal for a long lunch after the Byzantine Road hike or a visit to the Marathi marble quarries nearby.

Ververas Local Favorite

Traditional Greek · Naoussa

A family-run taverna with 4.9 stars from over 230 reviews — described by visitors as the best traditional food on Paros. Generous portions of home-cooked Greek staples at genuinely local prices. No fuss, no tourist menu.

Cash preferred. Arrive early as it fills quickly with locals and in-the-know visitors.

🐻

Berlin

Funky Fisch Bib Gourmand

Seafood · Asian-Western · Charlottenburg

The Michelin Bib Gourmand fish restaurant Berlin didn't know it needed. The centrepiece is a large fresh fish counter — you choose your fish, watch it prepared in the open kitchen, and eat it with Asian-influenced sauces and sides. The popcorn prawns, tuna on herb risotto, and the passion fruit crème brûlée are highlights. The atmosphere is lively and the space buzzes.

Kantstraße 135, Charlottenburg. Reservations recommended; closed Mondays and Sundays. The counter seats overlooking the kitchen are the best in the house.

Barra Bib Gourmand

Contemporary · Natural Wine · Neukölln

In Neukölln's Schillerkiez neighborhood, Barra earns its Bib Gourmand with ingredient-led contemporary cooking from regional farmers — celery and apple with Young Buck blue cheese; peas and radish with monkfish. No eccentricity for its own sake: the products are simple and the combinations are precise. Excellent natural wine list.

Okerstraße 2, Neukölln. A more local, quieter alternative to the Charlottenburg scene.

Cookies Cream Michelin Star

Vegetarian Fine Dining · Mitte

Since 2007, Berlin's best-known vegetarian fine dining restaurant — Michelin starred since 2018. The hidden entrance (through a parking garage, up a service staircase) adds to the mythology. Bold creative vegan and vegetarian cuisine that surprises at every turn. Near Friedrichstrasse.

Behrenstraße 55, Mitte. Book well in advance. Excellent for Lauren — a masterclass in plant-forward creative cuisine as a luxury positioning.

Brasserie Colette Bib Gourmand

French Brasserie · Schöneberg

Two-Michelin-star chef Tim Raue's more casual side — classic French brasserie dishes (steak frites, foie gras, entrecôte) with Raue's characteristic sharpness and a very well-stocked wine list. The Bib Gourmand recognizes the strong value proposition for the quality of cooking.

Passauer Straße 7, Schöneberg. Great for a celebratory last dinner before the flight home.

Historical Context

The stories behind the stones — arranged to deepen what you'll see in person.

Classical Period · 5th Century BC – 2nd Century AD

The Acropolis & the Birth of Democracy

Legend holds that Athena and Poseidon competed for Athens on this rock — Poseidon struck it to create a saltwater spring, but Athena's gift of the olive tree carried the vote. Below, the Ancient Agora was the beating commercial and democratic heart: a marketplace where the idea of citizens governing themselves was not just argued but tested, refined, and exported across the Mediterranean world.

"We do not copy our neighbours, but are an example to them."
— Pericles, Funeral Oration, 431 BC
Athens · Acropolis

Bronze Age · c. 1600 BC & Ptolemaic Period

Santorini: Volcanic Catastrophe & Island Garrisons

The island's crescent shape is the scar of the Minoan eruption — one of the largest volcanic events in recorded human history, which collapsed the entire center of the island into the sea and likely spawned tsunamis that reached Egypt. This catastrophe likely inspired the Atlantis myth. The high ridges that survived were later occupied by Ancient Thera, a mountaintop city that served as a strategic garrison for the Ptolemaic kings of Egypt and is still walkable today.

Santorini · Caldera

Antiquity · c. 5th Century BC onwards

Paros: The Island of Perfect Marble

In antiquity, Paros was the Saudi Arabia of sculptural marble. The underground quarries at Marathi produced "Parian lithos" — a uniquely semi-transparent white stone with an almost luminous quality — used for the Venus de Milo, the Nike of Paionios, and countless masterpieces now scattered across the world's museums. The Parian Chronicle, a massive marble stele engraved with a timeline of Greek history and mythology, is one of the most remarkable artifacts to survive antiquity.

Paros · Marble Quarries

Post-WWII · 1948–1989

Berlin: The Airlift, the Wall & Reunification

After WWII left Berlin divided among four Allied powers, the Soviets blockaded all land access in 1948. For 15 months, Western powers sustained the city entirely by air — "Operation Vittles" flew over 278,000 sorties delivering 2.3 million tons of supplies, including coal and flour. Later, the Berlin Wall (1961–1989) physically severed the city. Today the Gedenkstätte Berliner Mauer preserves the most authentic remaining section — watchtower, death strip, and escape tunnel documentation — far more affecting than the tourist-facing remnants at Checkpoint Charlie.

Berlin · Brandenburg Gate

For Erin (Aerospace Engineering): In Athens, the Antikythera Mechanism at the National Archaeological Museum is a 2,100-year-old analog computer — a bronze geared device that tracked astronomical cycles with a precision that astonished modern engineers when it was decoded. Arguably the most sophisticated engineering artifact of the ancient world. In Berlin, the German Technology Museum (Deutsches Technikmuseum) covers aviation history including early jet propulsion development.

For Lauren (International Fashion & Business): The Cycladic islands — Paros especially — have become a significant summer destination for European fashion industry figures, and Paros in particular has a small but growing artisan and jewelry design scene centered in Naoussa. In Berlin, the Hackesche Höfe (adjacent to your hotel) is a complex of interconnected courtyards housing independent fashion designers, concept stores, and galeries — one of the best concentrations of independent European fashion retail outside Paris.
